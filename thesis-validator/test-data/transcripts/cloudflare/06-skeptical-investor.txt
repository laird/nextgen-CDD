Expert Call Transcript - Investment Concerns
Date: December 15, 2024
Interviewee: Test Foster, Hedge Fund Portfolio Manager

Interviewer: What's your view on Cloudflare at current valuations?

Investor: Skeptical. The stock has been a great performer but the valuation assumes near-flawless execution for years. At 15-20x revenue, there's limited margin for error. One quarter of deceleration and the multiple compresses significantly.

Interviewer: What specific concerns do you have about the business?

Investor: Stock-based compensation is egregious. They're diluting shareholders by 5-6% annually. When you adjust for that, profitability is much further away than the non-GAAP numbers suggest. Management owns significant stock so they're not feeling the dilution pain.

Interviewer: What about the competitive moat?

Investor: The moat is narrower than bulls claim. CDN is largely commoditized. Their security products compete against well-funded specialists. Workers is innovative but AWS has Lamba@Edge and CloudFront Functions. The hyperscalers can replicate most of what Cloudflare does and bundle it for free.

Interviewer: Any positives you'd acknowledge?

Investor: The network scale is real and hard to replicate quickly. The developer community loyalty is genuine. And Matthew Prince is a visionary founder who's built something significant. If they can prove sustainable profitability while maintaining growth, the stock could work from here.

Interviewer: What would make you change your mind?

Investor: Show me consistent GAAP profitability without financial engineering. Show me that large customer growth can sustain above 25% as the base gets bigger. And show me that Workers is generating meaningful revenue, not just developer goodwill. Until then, there are better risk-reward setups in tech.
